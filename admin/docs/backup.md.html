How to Backup FSIP

You’ll need access to both your Web and database data to perform a complete backup of FSIP.

  1. Download your Web directory.
  2. Download your database contents.
Download your Web directory

Using an FTP, SFTP, WebDAV or other client, download your entire FSIP installation. For quicker backups, the essential files are config.php, /extensions, /includes, and /themes. Optionally, you may or may not want to backup /images (likely the largest directory) depending on whether you have copies of your images elsewhere.

Tip

If you have root access, compress your directory into an archive, and then download that archive. The download will be much faster.

tar -c your_directory --exclude "images" > your_archive.tar

Download your database contents

First, determine which database type you’re using by choosing Settings. Then follow the directions below for your database type.

MySQL

Go to your Web host’s control panel and look for an automated method to perform backups. You can also use phpMyAdmin, which is commonly offered, to download your database.

PostgreSQL

Go to your Web host’s control panel and look for an automated method to perform backups. You can also use pgAdmin, which is commonly offered, to download your database.

SQLite

Download the file /db/fsip_??????.db. That’s it.
